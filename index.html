Faq Global Facility

Testing your Android app can be more challenging than it seems. Here’s how hiring professional testers can save you time, effort, and headaches:

How does your testing work?

We run the testing on 12 separate Google accounts and devices. The devices are opted in for your app's testing program for at least 14 days. The testing involves interacting with your app in a human-like manner—pressing buttons, filling in inputs, etc.

What types of apps are eligible for testing?

We provide testers for all apps submitted to the Google Play Console, as long as they are functional and ready in their initial version.

Do you have a refund policy?

If you are not satisfied with our service, you can email your refund request to contact@12testersgoogleplay.com. We accept requests up to 5 days after purchase. It would be helpful if you also provide feedback on why you would like a refund, so we can improve the service for others in the future.

How do I prepare my app for testing?

You need to start a closed testing program in the Google Play Console. We will provide detailed instructions on how to set this up and how to add testers.

Do you guarantee that my app will be approved on Google Play?

Yes, we provide a guarantee that our testing service will help your app meet Google Play's requirements, and we will continue testing until your app is approved. Our team will work closely with you, offering reports and making sure your app passes all necessary checks for acceptance.

I am not familiar with this process, will you guide me on how to proceed?

Yes, we will provide you with all the guidance you need, including video tutorials, screenshots, and step-by-step instructions to help you understand the process and successfully pass the testing phase. We are here to support you throughout.

What sets your service apart from others?

We are a team of mobile developers who have developed hundreds of different apps and published them on various app stores. Therefore, we possess extensive knowledge and are well-versed in the procedures. Who better than a programmer themselves to test the app?
